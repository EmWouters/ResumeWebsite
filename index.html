About me

Solving complex problems through creative and "out of the box" thinking is my lifeblood. Once I grab on to a challenge, it's hard to let go. Days turn to weeks, weeks to months, months to years. Once I find my focus and motivation to do something difficult, I lose track of time. I am very passionate about art, music, coding, and science. The more I develop skills in these areas, the more I feel complete. That's not to say I don't have hobbies. I go out with friends, do occasional workouts, play a videogame once in a while, and find great relaxation listening to music. Whenever I travel, I make sure to seek out places of peace and tranquility.

I was born in 1987, and can't remember a period in my life were I wasn't trying to be creative. Now I'm in my mid 30's, married, and still seeking out difficult creative challenges. After having spend so much time learning how to code, and following a course, I realized coding is more than just a passion. It's a life goal, one that I can not neglect. I believe it is rare in life to find the very purpose of your being, and while I find myself lucky to have found this passion, I know also there is a lot of work to be done now. And yes, I certainly have the determination and the will to be the best I can be. Time to get to work!

Project: Little Lagoon

Research in oil separation techniques

After graduating a masters in chemistry and industrial engineering with distinction at the university of Antwerp, I went on to become a researcher at the BioGem research group at the university. There I had 2 projects. The main project was a research to separate and clean up oil waste from the sunflower oil industry (Due to an NDA, I can't mention the companies involved). With the purified fatty acids I went on to synthesize water-repelling aluminium soaps, which can be used as coating on metallic surfaces. Other materials I managed to separate was a nearly pure (>99%) fraction of sterols, and fractions with vitamine E & squalene in them. With each new design technique added to the overall separation process, the remaining vitamine E and squalene in the waste product became ever more concentrated. Squalene is an incredibly expensive product used primarily in the cosmetics industry, and it is usually recovered from shark livers or other fish.

A separate, secondary project was running in parallel, for which I was also responsible. This involved bleaching a brown saponine powder, to be used as an emulsifier. This too was a succes. Using ozone gas and a custom environmentally friendly solvent, I managed to bleach the dissolved powder, as well as fully recover it as dry material.
